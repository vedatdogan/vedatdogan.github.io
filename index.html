Research

My research develops AI-supported decision-making systems that solve complex hierarchical optimization problems in real-world applications. Using game-theoretic models and advanced machine learning, I create algorithms that help organizations make better strategic decisions in competitive and resource-constrained environments.

Current Research Interests

Bayesian Bilevel Optimization

Developing efficient Bayesian approaches for hierarchical optimization where upper-level decisions must account for lower-level responses. Using Gaussian processes and Bayesian methods to improve solution quality and computational efficiency in complex, multi-level decision problems.

Supply Chain Optimization Strategic Resource Allocation

Graph Neural Networks for Network Analysis

Leveraging graph neural networks to solve optimization and decision-making problems in network-structured systems. Developing methods for network reshaping, identifying influential nodes, and optimizing complex interconnected systems.

Network Reshaping Influential Node Identification Link Prediction

Game-Theoretic Decision Making

Building AI systems for strategic decision-making in competitive and collaborative settings. Creating algorithms that solve multi-level optimization problems in strategic contexts, with applications in diplomacy, military strategy, and competitive resource allocation.

Diplomatic Negotiations Military Strategy Competitive Optimization
